# Nurse Dashboard Guide

## Overview

The Nurse Dashboard is the central interface for healthcare professionals to review, manage, and act on AI-generated patient assessments. This guide provides comprehensive instructions for safe and effective use of the dashboard.

### Main Dashboard Components

#### Patient Queue
- **Waiting Assessments**: List of pending AI-generated assessments awaiting review
- **Priority Indicators**: Visual indicators for urgent or emergency cases
- **Time Stamps**: When each assessment was completed
- **Patient Demographics**: Age range and general information (non-identifying)

#### Assessment Details Panel
- **AI-Generated Summary**: Structured summary of patient symptoms and concerns
- **Risk Assessment**: AI-generated risk level and recommendations
- **Conversation Transcript**: Full conversation between patient and AI system
- **Follow-up Questions**: Areas requiring additional information

#### Action Panel
- **Accept Recommendations**: Approve AI-generated recommendations
- **Modify Recommendations**: Edit and adjust AI recommendations
- **Request Follow-up**: Request additional patient information
- **Escalate Care**: Escalate to physician or emergency services
- **Document Decision**: Add clinical notes and rationale

### Navigation Elements
- **Dashboard Home**: Main overview of all pending assessments
- **Patient Details**: Detailed view of individual assessments
- **Queue Management**: Manage and prioritize the assessment queue
- **Reports**: Generate reports on dashboard activity
- **Settings**: Configure dashboard preferences

## üìã Assessment Review Process

### Step 1: Initial Assessment Review

#### Review AI-Generated Information
1. **Read Complete Summary**: Review all information gathered by the AI
2. **Check Conversation**: Read through the patient-AI conversation
3. **Note Red Flags**: Identify any potential safety concerns
4. **Review Risk Level**: Note the AI-assigned risk level and rationale

#### Key Review Points
- **Completeness**: Is the assessment complete and comprehensive?
- **Accuracy**: Does the information seem accurate and consistent?
- **Safety**: Are there any safety concerns that need immediate attention?
- **Clarity**: Is the information clear and actionable?

### Step 2: Clinical Assessment

#### Independent Clinical Evaluation
1. **Compare with Experience**: Compare AI assessment with your clinical knowledge
2. **Identify Gaps**: Note any missing information or unclear areas
3. **Apply Clinical Judgment**: Use your expertise to evaluate the situation
4. **Consider Context**: Factor in clinical context and patient population

#### Assessment Questions to Consider
- Does the AI assessment align with your clinical expectations?
- Are there any red flags that might have been missed?
- What additional information would be helpful?
- What is your clinical impression of this case?

### Step 3: Decision Making

#### Determine Action
1. **Accept AI Recommendations**: If recommendations align with your clinical judgment
2. **Modify Recommendations**: Adjust recommendations based on clinical expertise
3. **Request Additional Information**: If more details are needed
4. **Escalate Care**: If immediate clinical intervention is needed

#### Documentation Requirements
- Document your review process
- Note any modifications to AI recommendations
- Provide clinical rationale for decisions
- Include any additional assessment findings

## üö® Emergency Protocol Integration

### Emergency Detection
The AI system flags potential emergency situations, but **always verify independently**:

#### Red Flag Symptoms Requiring Immediate Attention
- **Chest Pain**: Any chest pain or cardiac-related symptoms
- **Respiratory Distress**: Difficulty breathing, severe shortness of breath
- **Neurological Symptoms**: Stroke signs, severe headache, altered mental status
- **Severe Bleeding**: Uncontrolled bleeding or hemorrhagic shock
- **Altered Consciousness**: Severe dizziness, fainting, confusion
- **Severe Allergic Reactions**: Anaphylaxis symptoms

#### Emergency Response Protocol
1. **Stop Dashboard Review**: Immediately stop normal assessment review
2. **Direct Patient Contact**: Contact patient directly if possible
3. **Emergency Services**: Contact emergency services if indicated
4. **Document Emergency**: Document emergency protocol activation
5. **Follow-up**: Ensure appropriate follow-up care

### Emergency Dashboard Features
- **Emergency Alert Banner**: Prominent display for emergency cases
- **Quick Actions**: Rapid access to emergency response tools
- **Emergency Contacts**: Direct access to emergency service contacts
- **Location Information**: Patient location data for emergency response

## üë• Patient Management

### Triage Level Management

#### Understanding AI-Generated Triage Levels
- **IMMEDIATE (Level 1)**: Life-threatening conditions
- **EMERGENT (Level 2)**: Urgent conditions needing immediate attention
- **URGENT (Level 3)**: Care needed within 30 minutes
- **LESS_URGENT (Level 4)**: Care needed within 2 hours
- **NON-URGENT (Level 5)**: Care needed within 24 hours

#### Modifying Triage Levels
1. **Review AI Assessment**: Understand the rationale for AI triage level
2. **Apply Clinical Judgment**: Use your expertise to evaluate appropriateness
3. **Modify if Necessary**: Adjust triage level based on clinical assessment
4. **Document Changes**: Record rationale for any modifications

- **Answer Questions**: Address patient concerns and questions

#### Follow-up Procedures
