## üí¨ Starting Your Conversation

### Opening Screen
When you start a new consultation, you'll see:
1. **Welcome Message**: Brief introduction from the AI
2. **Consent Reminder**: Confirmation of your privacy and safety rights
3. **Emergency Information**: Quick access to emergency contacts
4. **Start Button**: Begin the conversation

### First Response
The AI will greet you and ask an open-ended question such as:
- "Hello! I'm here to help gather information about your health concerns. Can you tell me what brings you here today?"
- "Welcome! Please describe any symptoms or health issues you're experiencing."

## üìù How to Respond Effectively

### Best Practices for Your Responses

#### Be Specific and Detailed
- **Good**: "I've had a persistent headache for three days. It's a throbbing pain on the right side of my head, and it gets worse when I move."
- **Less Helpful**: "My head hurts."

#### Use Descriptive Language
- **Pain Types**: Sharp, dull, throbbing, stabbing, burning, aching
- **Severity**: Mild (1-3), moderate (4-6), severe (7-10)
- **Location**: Be specific about where you feel symptoms
- **Timing**: When did it start? How long does it last?

#### Include Context
- **Triggers**: What makes it better or worse?
- **Activities**: What were you doing when symptoms started?
- **Medications**: What have you taken for relief?
- **Previous Episodes**: Have you had similar symptoms before?

### Common Response Types

#### Describing Symptoms
- **Current State**: "Right now I feel..."
- **Comparison**: "This is different from..."
- **Changes Over Time**: "It started as... and now it's..."
- **Impact on Activities**: "I can't..."

#### Answering Follow-up Questions
- **Yes/No Questions**: Answer clearly
- **Multiple Choice**: Select the most accurate option
- **Scale Questions**: Use the 1-10 scale as directed
- **Time Questions**: Provide specific timeframes

#### Asking Your Own Questions
- **Clarification**: "Can you explain what you mean by...?"
- **Concerns**: "Is it normal to have...?"
- **Expectations**: "What should I expect next?"

## üéØ Question Types You'll Encounter

### Open-Ended Questions
These ask you to describe in your own words:
- "Tell me more about your symptoms"
- "How are you feeling today?"
- "What's been going on with your health?"

### Specific Detail Questions
These ask for particular information:
- "On a scale of 1-10, how severe is your pain?"
- "How long have you had this symptom?"
- "Where exactly do you feel the pain?"

### Yes/No Questions
Simple confirmations or denials:
- "Have you taken any medication for this?"
- "Do you have a fever?"
- "Have you experienced this before?"

### Multiple Choice Questions
Select from provided options:
- "Is your pain: a) Sharp, b) Dull, c) Throbbing, d) Burning?"
- "The symptom started: a) Suddenly, b) Gradually over hours, c) Gradually over days"

### Ranked Scale Questions
Rate on a numerical scale:
- "Rate your pain from 1 (no pain) to 10 (worst pain imaginable)"
- "How concerned are you about these symptoms from 1 (not concerned) to 10 (very concerned)?"

## üö® Emergency Detection

### Automatic Emergency Recognition
The AI is programmed to recognize emergency symptoms and will:
- **Stop Normal Questions**: Immediately halt routine assessment
- **Display Emergency Warning**: Show clear emergency instructions
- **Provide Emergency Contacts**: Give you emergency service numbers
- **Contact Emergency Services**: May automatically contact emergency services if required

### Red Flag Symptoms
The AI will immediately flag these symptoms as potential emergencies:
- **Chest Pain**: Any chest pain or pressure
- **Difficulty Breathing**: Trouble breathing or shortness of breath
- **Stroke Signs**: Facial drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty
- **Severe Bleeding**: Uncontrolled bleeding
- **Loss of Consciousness**: Fainting or severe dizziness
- **Severe Allergic Reaction**: Difficulty breathing after exposure to allergens

### Emergency Response Protocol
If emergency symptoms are detected:
1. **Immediate Alert**: "EMERGENCY SYMPTOMS DETECTED"
2. **Clear Instructions**: "Please contact emergency services immediately"
3. **Safety Questions**: "Are you alone? Do you have someone who can help?"
4. **Location Confirmation**: "Please confirm your current location"
5. **Follow-up Options**: "Would you like me to contact emergency services for you?"

## üìä Understanding Your Progress

### Assessment Stages

#### Stage 1: Initial Assessment (0-25%)
- **Welcome and Consent**: Introduction and privacy confirmation
- **Primary Complaint**: Main reason for seeking help
- **Basic Demographics**: Age range and general information

#### Stage 2: Symptom Details (25-50%)
- **Detailed Symptom Description**: In-depth symptom information
- **Symptom History**: When and how symptoms developed
- **Severity Assessment**: How severe symptoms are

#### Stage 3: Medical Context (50-75%)
- **Medical History**: Relevant past medical conditions
- **Current Medications**: Medications you're taking
- **Allergies**: Known allergies and reactions

#### Stage 4: Risk Assessment (75-100%)
- **Red Flag Screening**: Check for emergency symptoms
- **Risk Factors**: Identify any high-risk situations
- **Final Questions**: Last details needed for assessment

### Progress Indicators
- **Percentage Complete**: Shows overall progress
- **Stage Indicator**: Current stage of assessment
- **Estimated Time**: Approximate time remaining
- **Questions Remaining**: Number of questions left

## üìù After Your Assessment

### Assessment Completion
When your assessment is complete:
1. **Summary Display**: You'll see a summary of information gathered
2. **Confirmation**: You'll be asked to confirm the information
3. **Human Review**: Information is sent to healthcare professionals
4. **Follow-up Instructions**: You'll receive instructions for next steps

### Assessment Summary
The summary may include:
- **Primary Concerns**: Main health issues identified
- **Symptom Summary**: Key symptoms and their characteristics
- **Risk Level**: Overall risk assessment
- **Recommended Actions**: Suggested next steps

### Next Steps Options
- **Nurse Review**: Your information will be reviewed by a nurse
- **Doctor Consultation**: You may be scheduled with a doctor
- **Follow-up Questions**: Additional questions may be needed
- **Emergency Referral**: You may be referred for immediate care

## üí° Tips for Best Results

### Before Starting
- **Quiet Environment**: Find a quiet, private space
- **Good Lighting**: Ensure adequate lighting for any visual assessments
- **Comfortable Position**: Sit comfortably for the duration
- **Gather Information**: Have medication lists and medical history ready

### During the Conversation
- **Take Your Time**: Don't rush through responses
- **Be Honest**: Provide accurate, truthful information
- **Ask Questions**: If you don't understand, ask for clarification
- **Stay Focused**: Keep responses relevant to your health concerns

### Communication Best Practices
- **Clear Speech**: Speak clearly and at a normal pace
- **Specific Details**: Provide specific information when asked
- **Honest Responses**: Be truthful about your symptoms and concerns
- **Ask for Help**: Don't hesitate to ask for assistance

## üîí Privacy and Security During Use

### What Information to Share
- **Health Information**: Symptoms, medical history, medications
- **General Demographics**: Age range, general location (for emergency services)
- **Current Symptoms**: How you're feeling right now

### What NOT to Share
- **Personal Identifiers**: Name, address, phone number, email
- **Financial Information**: Insurance details, payment information
- **Social Security Number**: Never share this information
- **Identity Documents**: Don't share photos of identification

### Security Reminders
- **Private Environment**: Ensure privacy during your consultation
- **Secure Connection**: Use secure, private internet connections
- **Log Out**: Always log out when finished
- **Report Issues**: Report any privacy or security concerns
